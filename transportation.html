Getting to and around Taniti is easy with our comprehensive transportation options.

Transportation

Getting to Taniti

Taniti is accessible by both air and sea, making it convenient for travelers from around the world to reach our island paradise.

By Air: Taniti has a small but modern airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. The airport is located just outside Taniti City, approximately 10 miles from most major hotels and resorts. Currently, Taniti is expanding its airport to accommodate larger jets, which will allow for more frequent flights and direct connections from major international airports.

Regular flights connect Taniti to several Pacific destinations, with connections available to major international hubs. Flight schedules vary by season, with more frequent service during peak tourist months. Most visitors find it convenient to book flights through travel agents or online booking platforms that specialize in Pacific island destinations.

By Sea: A small cruise ship docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week, offering another way to visit Taniti. The cruise ship terminal is located in Taniti City, within walking distance of many hotels, restaurants, and shops. Some visitors choose to combine a cruise with an extended stay on the island.

Private yachts and sailboats also visit Taniti regularly, with several marinas and anchorages available around the island. The main marina in Yellow Leaf Bay offers full services including fuel, water, electricity, and provisioning.

Getting Around Taniti

Once you've arrived on Taniti, you'll find several convenient options for exploring the island.

Public Buses: Taniti has a public bus system that serves Taniti City and the surrounding areas. The buses run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily, making it easy to get around without a car. Bus stops are clearly marked, and routes cover most tourist areas, beaches, and major attractions. The bus system is affordable and provides a great way to experience local life while getting where you need to go.

Rental Cars: Several rental car agencies operate on Taniti, offering a range of vehicles from compact cars to SUVs. Renting a car gives you the freedom to explore the island at your own pace and visit more remote locations. Most rental agencies are located near the airport and in Taniti City, and many hotels can arrange rentals for their guests.

Driving on Taniti is relatively easy, as most roads are well-maintained and traffic is generally light outside of Taniti City. The speed limit in Taniti City is 25 mph, and 40 mph outside the city. Visitors should note that driving is on the right side of the road, and an international driver's license is recommended.

Taxis: Taxis are readily available throughout Taniti, particularly in Taniti City and at the airport, hotels, and major tourist areas. Taxis are a convenient option for getting to specific destinations or when you don't want to drive. Most taxi drivers are knowledgeable about the island and can provide recommendations for restaurants, activities, and attractions.

Bicycle Rentals: For the more adventurous and environmentally conscious traveler, bicycle rentals are available at several locations throughout the island. Biking is a popular way to explore Taniti, especially in the flatter southern and eastern regions. Many visitors enjoy the slower pace and the ability to stop and explore interesting spots along the way.

Walking: Taniti City is compact and very walkable, with most hotels, restaurants, shops, and attractions within easy walking distance of each other. Many visitors find that they can explore the city on foot and only need transportation for trips to beaches, the volcano, or other outlying areas.

Tour Buses: Several tour companies offer guided bus tours that take visitors to major attractions around the island. These tours are a great option for first-time visitors who want to see the highlights and learn about Taniti's history and culture from knowledgeable guides. Tour buses typically visit the volcano viewing area, beaches, rainforest trails, and cultural sites.

Transportation Tips

When planning your transportation on Taniti, consider that the island is relatively small, and most destinations are within a 30-45 minute drive from Taniti City. Many visitors find that a combination of walking, public buses, and occasional taxis or rental cars works well for their needs.

If you're staying at a resort, many offer complimentary shuttle services to Taniti City and popular beaches. Check with your accommodation about available transportation services when making your reservation.

For those planning to explore extensively, a rental car provides the most flexibility. However, if you're primarily staying in Taniti City and visiting nearby beaches, the public bus system and taxis may be all you need.
