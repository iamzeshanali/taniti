Food & Lodging

Accommodations

Taniti offers a wide range of lodging options to suit every preference and budget. From luxury beachfront resorts to charming bed and breakfasts, you'll find the perfect place to call home during your stay.

Luxury Resorts: Taniti is home to several world-class resorts featuring infinity pools, private beaches, spa facilities, and fine dining restaurants. These resorts offer all-inclusive packages and are perfect for those seeking a pampered, worry-free vacation experience. Most luxury resorts are located along the eastern coast, offering stunning ocean views and easy access to the best beaches.

Hotels: The island has numerous hotels ranging from mid-range to upscale options. Many hotels are located in Taniti City, providing convenient access to shopping, restaurants, and nightlife. These establishments offer comfortable rooms, on-site dining, and helpful concierge services to help you plan your activities.

Bed & Breakfasts: For a more intimate and authentic experience, consider staying at one of Taniti's charming bed and breakfasts. These family-run establishments offer personalized service, home-cooked breakfasts, and the opportunity to connect with local hosts who can share insider tips about the island.

Vacation Rentals: Several private vacation rentals are available throughout the island, from beachfront villas to cozy cottages in the hills. These properties offer privacy, full kitchens, and the flexibility to create your own schedule. Many rentals are managed by local property management companies that can assist with booking and provide recommendations.

Hostels: Budget-conscious travelers will find several hostels in Taniti City offering affordable dormitory-style accommodations. These hostels provide basic amenities, communal kitchens, and social spaces where you can meet other travelers.

Dining & Cuisine

Taniti's culinary scene is a delightful fusion of traditional Polynesian flavors, fresh local ingredients, and international influences. The island's restaurants range from casual beachside cafes to upscale fine dining establishments.

Local Cuisine: Traditional Tanitian cuisine features fresh seafood, tropical fruits, root vegetables, and coconut-based dishes. Must-try local specialties include fresh-caught fish prepared in traditional methods, taro-based dishes, and tropical fruit salads. Many restaurants offer "catch of the day" menus featuring fish caught that morning by local fishermen.

International Options: Taniti's diverse population has brought a variety of international cuisines to the island. You'll find excellent Asian fusion restaurants, American-style diners, Italian trattorias, and more. Most restaurants in tourist areas offer menus in English, and many cater to various dietary preferences including vegetarian, vegan, and gluten-free options.

Casual Dining: Throughout the island, you'll find numerous casual restaurants and cafes perfect for a relaxed meal. Many beachside establishments offer simple but delicious fare, from fresh fish tacos to tropical smoothies. These spots are perfect for lunch between activities or a casual dinner with ocean views.

Fine Dining: Several upscale restaurants offer sophisticated dining experiences with creative menus featuring local ingredients prepared with international techniques. These establishments often require reservations and provide elegant atmospheres perfect for special occasions or romantic dinners.

Local Markets & Street Food: For an authentic experience, visit Taniti City's local market where vendors sell fresh produce, prepared foods, and local snacks. The market is a great place to sample traditional Tanitian street food and interact with local vendors. Many visitors enjoy picking up fresh fruits, local pastries, and other treats to enjoy throughout the day.

Grocery Stores: Taniti has two supermarkets in Taniti City that stock a wide variety of groceries, including fresh produce, meats, seafood, and international products. Smaller grocery stores are located in villages throughout the island. If you're staying in a vacation rental, you'll have everything you need to prepare your own meals using fresh local ingredients.

Most restaurants on Taniti accept major credit cards, though some smaller establishments and local markets may prefer cash. Tipping is appreciated but not always expected, as service charges are sometimes included in the bill at upscale establishments. The island's restaurants generally maintain high standards of food safety and hygiene.
